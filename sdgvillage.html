What is the SDG Village?

The SDGs Village at PDF 2025 serves as a platform for dialogue on the localization and implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Bringing together policymakers, community implementers, civil society organizations, and development partners, the village fosters discussions on adapting global SDG targets to local contexts to ensure inclusive, relevant, and effective implementation. Under the theme “Dialogue as a Driver for the Sustainable Implementation of the Development Goals,” the sessions focus on how individuals and institutions can use dialogue to drive sustainable change. As the 2030 deadline approaches, the importance of localized approaches in achieving tangible progress remains a key focus.

The village incorporates various interactive approaches to engage participants, including panel discussions that strengthen multi-level governance, artivism that blends artistic expression with activism, and a data and monitoring masterclass that emphasizes the need for accurate data collection and disaggregation. Policy and governance dialogues provide a space for engagement between government and civil society, while an eco-fashion show highlights SDG 12 by promoting sustainable consumption and production. Additionally, the fishbowl approach encourages inclusive conversations between duty-bearers and citizens, ensuring diverse perspectives are heard.
